Subrecipients and Recipients (Pass-Through Entities) Responsibilities  \n\nThe recipient is the entity that receives a federal award directly from HRSA.  \n\nThe recipient can give a portion of the direct award to other organizations, called subrecipients, to accomplish the goals and objectives of the award.  \n\nIn this case, the recipient becomes a pass-through entity and the subrecipient’s award is called a subaward.  \n\nAs a recipient, you must ensure the applicable general terms and conditions stated in this document flow down to subrecipients. See HHS Administrative and National Policy Requirements.  \n\nd. Prior Approval  \n\nYou must get prior approval for certain items, as stated in 45 CFR $\\S$ 75.308 and § 75.407. This includes significant rebudgeting of project costs.  \n\nSignificant budget changes require prior approval when they constitute a change of scope or exceed 25 percent of total costs or $\\mathbb{\\Phi}250{,}000$ , whichever is less, of the most recent approved award (budget period). Prior approval will also be required when rebudgeting includes  \n\nfunding into a direct cost category not previously requested. If you are not sure your budget   \nchange is beyond the scope, contact your Grants Management Specialist (GMS).   \nWe do not automatically authorize prior approvals under 45 CFR § 75.308(d): o We do not waive cost-related or administrative prior approvals unless specifically stated in your NOA. You must submit these as a Prior Approval action via HRSA Electronic Handbooks (EHBs). o Only responses to prior approval requests signed by the Grants Management Officer (GMO) are valid.  \n\nIf you act based on responses from other officials, you do so at your own risk. We do not consider these responses binding.  \n\ne. Indirect Costs  \n\nWhen HHS is the Federal Cognizant Agency or when HHS is acting as the shared-service provider for another Federal Cognizant Agency, HHS will issue [Negotiated Indirect Cost Agreements] (NICRAs) that incorporate the HHS SRL, to comply with HHS Appropriations Act requirement.  \n\nIf you do not have an approved indirect cost rate that complies with the HHS SRL requirement, you must take and document the following actions:  \n\nIdentify all HHS awards where HHS funds are used to pay any salary that exceeds the SRL.
